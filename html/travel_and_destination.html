Places to go in Kenya

We offer preventive health and wellness benefits designed to help maintain and improve our clients behaviour to achieve better health and reduce health risks.

Maasai Mara

Rolling hills, vast savannahs, stunning river crossings, and, of course, more wildlife and adventure than any film could possibly depict Welcome to the Masai Mara, home to the lions from 'Big Cat Diary,' the Great Migration's temporary home, formidable Maasai warriors, and some of the world's most magnificent safari resorts.

Nairobi National Park

A short drive out of Nairobi`s crucial commercial enterprise district is the Nairobi National Park. Wide open grass plains and backdrop of the city's scrapers, scattered acacia bush play host to a extensive sort of natural world along with the endangered black rhino, lions, leopards, cheetahs, hyenas, buffaloes, giraffes and numerous birdlife with over four hundred species recorded.

Lamu Archipelago

Lamu, the best beach in Kenya, has a living African-Arabic coastal tradition and world class houses and villas. Apart from the usual beach holidays pastimes, the activities includes sailing down the Kenyan coast in traditioanl dhows, swimming with dolphins, historical trips to museums, excavations and ancient sites, and visiting Lamu town.

Sera Wildlife Conservacy

Sera Wildlife Conservancy, is home to Sera Rhino Sanctuary, East Africa's first community Conservancy. It's a place that seeks to balance wildlife conservation, natural resources development and community development to protect livelihood diversification and culture.

Northern Frontier District

The Northern Frontier District is one of the most isolated and remote areas on earth. It is a holy grail for seasoned purveyors of African game and adventurers seeking forgotten worlds. The surrounding Lake Turkana, a breathtaking shore longer than the entire Kenyan coastline.

Laikipia Central Highlands

This Kenya's prime agtricultutral land, juxtaposed areas of human habitation, cattle husbandry, crop production and zones of wildlife habitats. It is a place you discover one of the most intimate and authentic places to stay in East Africa. One of the world's great animal research projects is based here.
